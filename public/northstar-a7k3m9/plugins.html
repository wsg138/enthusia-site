World & survival

Permanent World

The world is permanent. Bases, farms, tunnels, ruins, trade routes, and old fights can build up over time instead of disappearing on a season timer.

No land claimsPlayers can build anywhere, but they are responsible for hiding, defending, or rebuilding what they make.
Mostly vanilla wildernessThe main survival world stays close to vanilla.
Large OverworldThe -100k to +100k border gives players enough distance for travel, base hunting, and long-term projects.

PvP & danger

PvP Areas

PvP is part of the server, but it is not only one thing. Some areas are built for cleaner fights, while the wilderness keeps the risk that makes survival interesting.

Main Warzone

Sword, mace, spear, and regular combat. No building or breaking.

Cobweb Box

Utility PvP with limited placed items such as cobwebs, water, buttons, and pressure plates.

Warpit

Building, breaking, carts, explosives, traps, digging, and hole fighting. Resets every few hours.

Wilderness

Full vanilla PvP outside the Warzone, including crystals, anchors, TNT, pearls, and normal combat.

Economy & Market

Raw Gold Economy

The economy runs on raw gold. Players can keep gold in balance, withdraw it, rent Market stalls, and set up trades with other players.

  • Trading is mainly player-run and item-for-item.
  • Guild stalls are part of the long-term social economy.
  • No taxes.

Guilds

Guilds

Guilds give teams a real identity without turning Enthusia into factions. A good guild should feel like a known group on the server, not just a tag before a name.

Player-made teamsGuilds help groups build a name that other players recognize.
Custom progressionGroups can grow over time instead of being just a chat tag.
Market presenceGuilds can become part of trade, KOTH, and server lore.
